Disobey 2018 - Phishing through Email" by Niklas Särökaari

This YouTube video discusses phishing attacks carried out through email. In the video, Niklas Särökaari talks about various aspects of email phishing attacks, including how these attacks are performed, common tactics employed by cybercriminals, and ways to mitigate such threats. Phishing is a technique used by malicious actors to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information by posing as a legitimate entity via email.

Reasons People Click on Phishing Links

His presentation highlighted the results why people click on phishing links in a research entitled "Blackhat 2016: Exploiting Curiosity and Context - How to Make People Click on a Dangerous Link Despite Their Security Awareness:

  • Curiosity (34%): People tend to click on links out of curiosity, wanting to explore what lies behind them.
  • Expecting Similar Content (30%): If individuals expect something similar to what they are familiar with, they are more likely to click on a link.
  • Familiar Sender (16%): Attackers often use common names to appear familiar to recipients, increasing the likelihood of clicks.

Protection Mechanisms and Bypass

  • Sender Policy Framework (SPF): SPF is configured to verify the authenticity of the sender's email address. It checks whether the email was sent from an authorized server.
  • Domain Keys Identified Mail (DKIM): DKIM provides email validation and authentication. It ensures that the email content has not been tampered with during transit.
  • Domain Message Authentication Reporting and Conformance (DMARC): DMARC combines SPF and DKIM to make a domain more reputable and legitimate. It enforces alignment checks for both SPF and DKIM, enhancing email security.
  • Filtering:
    • Malware and Spam Filtering: These filters protect against emails containing malicious attachments or spam content.
    • URL Whitelisting/Web Filtering: Controls are implemented to allow only trusted URLs and filter out malicious ones.
    • Greylisting: A mechanism that temporarily rejects emails to combat spam and phishing.
  • Site Cloning: Cybercriminals clone legitimate websites to harvest credentials and deceive users.
  • IDN Homograph Attack: Phishers use punycode or Unicode to register domains that appear legitimate but are malicious.

Effective Phishing Strategies

Phishing campaigns often succeed due to the following strategies:

  • Package Delivery Service: Deceptive emails related to package deliveries are commonly effective.
  • Impersonation of Service Desks, IT Departments, or Technical Support: Recipients are more likely to trust emails from these entities.
  • Impersonation of Legitimate Sites (Gmail, LinkedIn, etc.): Emails that mimic trusted sites deceive recipients.
  • Relevance to the Company (Campaigns, Marketing, etc.): Phishing campaigns tailored to a company's activities are more likely to succeed.

Recommended Actions

To protect against phishing attacks:

  • Implement SPF, DKIM, and DMARC: Strengthen email authentication and validation.
  • Domain Security: Ensure your domain is protected from cybercriminals.
  • User Education and Training: Educate and train users to recognize phishing attempts.
  • Secure Infrastructure: Maintain a secure IT infrastructure to prevent unauthorized access.
